The problem

Most export delays don't happen at the port. They happen before the shipment is even booked.

01

Wrong or vague HS code. A misclassified product means the wrong duty rate, the wrong paperwork, or a customs query that holds up the consignment.

02

Missing documents. Each product and destination has a different document list — found out, usually, after the freight forwarder asks for something you don't have.

03

Margin discovered too late. Duty, freight, and compliance costs often get added up after the order is confirmed, not before.
